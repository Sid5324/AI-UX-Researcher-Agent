## PURPOSE

### What you're making?

An autonomous AI agent that independently conducts product research from start to finish. Product managers provide a high-level goal (like "understand why users are churning"), and the agent autonomously gathers data, recruits participants, runs experiments, and delivers validated recommendations—operating 24/7 and checking in only at critical decision points.

**Product Name:** Agentic Research AI

**Product Category:** Autonomous AI Product Intelligence Platform

**Goal:** Enable product managers to delegate complete research workflows to an AI agent that executes independently, reducing research time from weeks to days while improving decision quality through rigorous multi-method validation.

**Target Customer:** Product Managers at seed to Series B startups (50-500 employees) who need evidence-based decisions quickly but lack dedicated research resources.

**Statement of Need:** Product managers spend 40-60% of their time coordinating research tools and executing manual tasks rather than making strategic decisions. Current AI tools only assist—they don't execute autonomously. This creates a bottleneck where research either gets skipped (risky) or delays product development by 2-3 weeks per decision cycle.

**Key Benefits:**
- Research completes in 3-5 days instead of 15-20 days
- PM active time reduced from 50+ hours to <1 hour per research project
- Multi-method validation (surveys, interviews, A/B tests) ensures high confidence
- Agent learns from every project, becoming smarter over time
- All insights stored in searchable knowledge base for future reference

**Compelling Reasons to Buy/Use:**
- **Delegation not assistance:** Set a goal, agent handles everything autonomously
- **Compound intelligence:** Agent gets better at your specific product domain with each project
- **Evidence-based decisions:** Statistical confidence scores and multi-source validation
- **Time leverage:** 100x multiplier (agent works 24/7, user approves 2-3 checkpoints only)
- **No tool switching:** Single system replaces 5-10 separate research tools

### Why build it?

**Background: What led to prioritizing this product?**

Product managers at startups face a critical bottleneck: they need research to make good decisions, but research execution is painfully slow and manual. Current workflow requires PMs to:
- Manually switch between 5-10 tools (analytics, recruitment, analysis, documentation)
- Spend 40-60% of time on coordination and execution vs. strategic thinking
- Wait 15-20 days per research cycle (recruitment alone takes 5-7 days)
- Manually synthesize data from multiple sources
- Re-learn insights every project (no knowledge compounding)

This creates a false choice: skip research (risky) or delay product development (lose competitive advantage).

Existing solutions (Maze, UserTesting, Dovetail) are assistive tools—they help PMs execute faster but still require step-by-step manual direction. No solution offers true autonomous execution where the PM delegates a goal and the system independently completes the work.

With LLM capabilities (reasoning, tool use, self-correction) now mature enough for agentic workflows, we can finally build an AI that operates like a senior researcher on the team—not just a productivity tool.

**Assumptions: What assumptions are we making about users?**

1. **Trust in AI autonomy:** PMs will trust an AI agent to execute research independently if they can see the reasoning trail and approve at critical checkpoints
   - *Validation:* Prototype testing with 15 PMs showed 87% trusted agent after first successful goal
   
2. **Willingness to pay for time savings:** PMs value their time at $100-200/hour and will pay $79-199/month to save 20+ hours per project
   - *Validation:* 83% of interviewed PMs (n=50) said they'd pay for autonomous research

3. **Quality threshold:** Research quality from AI agents can match or exceed manual research when multi-method validation is used
   - *Validation:* Prototype tests showed agent-generated insights had 92% agreement with manual research baseline

4. **Checkpoint model works:** PMs prefer infrequent checkpoints (2-3 per project) over constant supervision
   - *Validation:* Beta testers rated checkpoint model 8.5/10 vs. step-by-step model 5.2/10

5. **Learning compounds value:** The product gets significantly more valuable as the research vault grows
   - *Hypothesis:* 10th project should complete 2-3x faster than 1st project due to accumulated knowledge

**Market Opportunities: Why now?**

**Timing is critical—three converging trends make this the right moment:**

1. **LLM Agent Capabilities Mature (2024-2026)**
   - GPT-4/Claude can now reason, use tools, self-correct—foundational for autonomy
   - Function calling and ReAct patterns proven in production
   - Cost decreased 10x (makes autonomous multi-day workflows economically viable)

2. **Research Tool Fragmentation at Peak**
   - Average PM uses 7-12 tools for research (Mixpanel, Amplitude, Notion, Figma, User Interviews, Maze, Dovetail, etc.)
   - Consolidation wave happening (companies want unified platforms)
   - $8B UX research tools market growing 15% annually
   - No dominant player owns end-to-end workflow

3. **PM Role Expanding, Resources Shrinking**
   - 2023-2025 layoffs hit research teams hardest
   - PMs now responsible for research but lack dedicated support
   - 180,000+ PMs in US tech companies
   - Each spends $10-15K annually on research tools/services

**Market Validation:**
- Interviewed 50 PMs: 82% cite research coordination as #1 time sink
- 76% use 5+ tools per research project (tool fatigue)
- 68% miss deadlines due to research delays
- Average research project costs $1,200 and takes 18 days
- Total addressable market: 180K PMs × $2K/year = $360M

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Maze, UserTesting, Dovetail:** Assistive tools (PMs still do the work)
- **GPT-4, Claude:** General purpose (not specialized for product research)
- **AutoGPT, BabyAGI:** General agents (brittle, no domain expertise)
- **Gap:** No one offers autonomous, goal-driven, self-correcting product research with domain specialization

**Opportunity:** First-mover advantage in agentic product research. Winner likely captures significant market share through network effects (research vault learning compounds).

### 2. Problems We Are Solving [Needs, Problems, Jobs to be Done]

**Problem #1: Research Takes Too Long**

**Current State:**
- Average research project: 15-20 days from hypothesis to recommendation
- PM spends 40-50 hours of active work coordinating and executing
- Breakdown:
  - Data gathering: 6 hours (manual pulls from 3-4 tools)
  - Participant recruitment: 5-7 days (waiting for signups)
  - Interview execution: 12 hours (8 interviews × 1.5 hours each)
  - Analysis: 8 hours (manual transcription, theme clustering)
  - Solution design: 10 hours (mockups, PRD writing)
  - Validation: 3-5 days (survey or prototype test)
  - Stakeholder buy-in: 4 hours (presentations, revisions)

**Impact:**
- Product launches delayed by 3-4 weeks per feature
- Competitors ship faster (window of opportunity closes)
- Revenue lost due to delayed improvements
- PM burnout from constant coordination work

**Jobs to be Done:**
*"When I need to understand a product problem, I want rapid, validated insights so I can make confident decisions without delaying the roadmap."*

**Success Criteria:**
- Research cycle: 15-20 days → 3-5 days (75% reduction)
- PM active time: 50 hours → <5 hours (90% reduction)
- Maintains or improves quality (multi-method validation)

---

**Problem #2: Tool Coordination Overhead**

**Current State:**
- PM uses 7-12 tools for single research project:
  1. Mixpanel/Amplitude (analytics)
  2. User Interviews (recruitment)
  3. Calendly (scheduling)
  4. Zoom (interviews)
  5. Otter.ai (transcription)
  6. Notion (documentation)
  7. Miro (affinity mapping)
  8. Figma (mockups)
  9. Typeform (surveys)
  10. Maze (prototype testing)
  11. Jira (implementation specs)
  12. Slack (team updates)

**Pain Points:**
- Each tool requires learning, setup, maintenance
- Data silos—manual copy-paste between tools
- 30-40% of time spent on tool coordination vs. thinking
- Context-switching destroys focus and productivity
- Tool costs accumulate ($200-500/month per PM)

**Impact:**
- Cognitive overhead reduces strategic thinking capacity
- Errors from manual data transfer
- Subscription fatigue (team consolidating tools)
- Onboarding friction (new PMs must learn entire stack)

**Jobs to be Done:**
*"When I start research, I want one system that handles everything autonomously so I don't waste mental energy coordinating tools."*

**Success Criteria:**
- Tool count: 7-12 tools → 1 unified system
- Context-switches: 50+ per project → 0 (except checkpoints)
- Tool costs: $200-500/month → $79-199/month
- Onboarding time: 2 weeks to learn tools → 1 hour

**Problem #3: No Compounding Knowledge**

**Current State:**
- Every research project starts from zero
- Previous insights buried in old Notion docs
- No systematic way to build on past learnings
- Same user behaviors validated repeatedly across projects
- No pattern detection across projects

**Example Scenario:**
- **January:** Research shows onboarding Step 3 causes confusion (validated with 10 interviews)
- **March:** New PM joins, researches same issue, interviews 8 more users, learns same thing
- **May:** Related feature launched, encounters same Step 3 pattern, researches again

Result: 3x redundant research, wasted time and money, users annoyed by repeated interview requests

**Impact:**
- Research velocity doesn't improve over time
- Organizational knowledge lost when PMs leave
- Budget wasted on redundant validation
- Users frustrated by repetitive research requests

**Jobs to be Done:**
*"When I start new research, I want to leverage everything my team already learned so we build on past knowledge, not repeat it."*

**Success Criteria:**
- Knowledge retention: 0% (lost in docs) → 90% (searchable vault)
- Redundant research: 30-40% of projects → <5%
- Research velocity: Flat over time → 2-3x faster by project #10
- Cross-PM knowledge sharing: Manual → Automatic

---

**Problem #4: Manual Execution Burden**

**Current State:**
- Current AI tools (GPT-4, Claude) only suggest—don't execute
- "AI copilots" still require human to drive every step
- Typical flow with AI assistant:
  1. User: "Help me research activation issues"
  2. AI: "You should pull Mixpanel data. Here's what to look for."
  3. User: [Manually opens Mixpanel, pulls data, copies to doc]
  4. AI: "Now you should recruit users. Use User Interviews."
  5. User: [Manually creates study, waits for signups, schedules]
  6. AI: "Now conduct interviews. Here are questions."
  7. User: [Manually interviews 8 users over 3 days]
  8. ...continues for 20+ steps

Result: AI shifted work from "manual research" to "manual research with suggestions"—marginal time savings, same bottleneck.

**Impact:**
- PM is still the bottleneck (must execute every step)
- AI tools feel like extra work (supervision overhead)
- Time savings: 10-20% (not the 10x promised)
- PM burnout continues

**Jobs to be Done:**
*"When I have a research goal, I want AI to autonomously execute the work and check in only at decision points so I can focus on strategic thinking."*

**Success Criteria:**
- PM involvement: 50 hours → <5 hours (90% reduction)
- Autonomous actions: 0% → 70%+ (agent executes without approval)
- Checkpoints: 20+ manual steps → 2-3 strategic approvals
- Time to solution: 20 days → 3-5 days

---

**Problem #5: Low Stakeholder Confidence in Research**

**Current State:**
- PM research often single-method (just interviews OR just analytics)
- Small sample sizes (5-10 users) due to time constraints
- No statistical confidence scores
- Stakeholders (CEO, VP Eng) question rigor
- Common pushback:
  - "That's just 8 people, not statistically significant"
  - "Did you validate this with data or is it anecdotal?"
  - "How do we know this will work at scale?"

**Impact:**
- Research-backed recommendations rejected 40% of the time
- Decisions revert to HiPPO (Highest Paid Person's Opinion)
- PM credibility damaged
- Features ship without validation (higher risk)

**Jobs to be Done:**
*"When I present research findings, I want quantified confidence scores and multi-method validation so stakeholders trust my recommendations."*

**Success Criteria:**
- Rejection rate: 40% → <15%
- Multi-method validation: 20% of projects → 90%
- Statistical confidence: Rarely provided → Always included
- Stakeholder trust score: 6.5/10 → 8.5/10

### DAY 8-11: Conduct Interviews (Manual, 16 hours PM time)

**Each Interview Flow (2 hours × 8 = 16 hours):**

**Example: Interview #1 (Day 8, 10:00 AM)**

- Review participant profile
- Open interview script in Notion (written yesterday)
- Start Zoom meeting
- Start Otter.ai recording

**10:03 AM - Conduct Interview (35 min)**
- Introduction and consent (5 min)
- Background questions (10 min)

  - "Walk me through signing up for our product"
  - "What happened when you got to the OAuth screen?"

  - "Why did you decide not to proceed?"

**10:38 AM - Post-Interview Work (45 min)**
- Thank participant, end call
- Review Otter.ai transcript (auto-generated, but needs cleanup)

## SOLUTION

### What is it?

Agentic Research AI is an autonomous AI agent system that operates on a continuous **Think → Act → Observe → Learn loop** to independently pursue product research goals from start to finish. Product managers provide a high-level objective (like "understand why users are churning"), and the agent autonomously:

- Gathers data from multiple sources (analytics, support tickets, reviews)
- Recruits and interviews users
- Analyzes patterns and forms hypotheses
- Designs and runs validation experiments  
- Generates implementation-ready deliverables (PRD, mockups, tickets)
- Monitors success post-launch

The agent checks in with the PM only at 2-3 critical decision points (like choosing between solution options) rather than requiring step-by-step supervision. This enables true **delegation** rather than mere **assistance**.

**Core Interaction Model:**

**Input:** Natural language goal  
*"Fix our activation rate decline. Budget $2K, timeline 1 week."*

**Execution:** Agent works autonomously for 3-5 days  
Agent reasoning, tool execution, self-correction all happen without PM involvement

**Checkpoints:** PM approves 2-3 strategic decisions  
*"Choose between solution A, B, or C"*

**Output:** Complete implementation package  
PRD, mockups, validated recommendation, Jira tickets, metrics dashboard

```python
def analyze_activation_funnel(funnel_id, date_range):
    """
    Agent pulls funnel data and interprets
    """
    
    # Call Mixpanel API
    response = requests.get(
        'https://mixpanel.com/api/2.0/funnels',
        auth=(api_key, ''),
        params={
            'funnel_id': funnel_id,
            'from_date': date_range['start'],
            'to_date': date_range['end']
        }
    )
    
    data = response.json()
    
    # Agent interprets data
    steps = data['data']['steps']
    drop_offs = []
    
    for i in range(len(steps) - 1):
        drop_rate = 1 - (steps[i+1]['count'] / steps[i]['count'])
        if drop_rate > 0.3:  # >30% drop is significant
            drop_offs.append({
                'from_step': steps[i]['event'],
                'to_step': steps[i+1]['event'],
                'drop_rate': drop_rate,
                'severity': 'high' if drop_rate > 0.5 else 'medium'
            })
    
    return {
        'funnel': steps,
        'drop_offs': drop_offs,
        'interpretation': generate_interpretation(drop_offs)
    }
```

**Agent:**
- Pulls data automatically
- Identifies drop-off points
- Interprets significance
- Forms hypotheses

**User sees:** *"Found 60% drop at OAuth step (high severity)"*

**Tool Execution Features:**

**Error Handling:**
```python
try:
    result = execute_tool(tool, params)
except APIRateLimitError:
    wait(60)  # Wait and retry
    result = execute_tool_with_backoff(tool, params)
except AuthenticationError:
    notify_user(f"Please reconnect {tool.name} in Settings")
    try_fallback_tool()
except ToolNotAvailableError:
    replan_without_tool()
```

**Cost Tracking:**
```python
for action in actions:
    cost = estimate_cost(action)
    if budget_tracker.can_afford(cost):
        execute(action)
        budget_tracker.record(cost)
    else:
        escalate_to_user(f"Action costs ${cost}, over budget")
```

**Permission System:**

Expensive/risky actions require approval:
```python
if action.cost > 500 or action.external_communication:
    approval = request_user_approval(action)
    if approval.granted:
        execute(action)
    else:
        replan_with_feedback(approval.feedback)
```
